## Template Structure {#template}

The Customer Retention Canvas (CRC) maps each use case through the full retention journey — from problem to habit formation. Fill in one column per use case.

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Use Case** | The specific way a user segment uses your product |
| **Problem** | The core pain point this use case solves |
| **Persona** | Who experiences this problem (demographics, role, context) |
| **Why** | Why your product solves this better than alternatives |
| **Alternative** | What they do today without your product |
| **Frequency** | How often they need this solved (Daily/Weekly/Monthly/Yearly) |
| **Retention Metric** | The time-based active metric matching their frequency (DAU/WAU/MAU/YAU) |
| **Setup Moment** | The first experience where the user gets the product configured for their need |
| **Setup Metric** | Quantitative measure: [action] within [timeframe] |
| **Aha Moment** | The first time the user experiences real value |
| **Aha Moment Metric** | Quantitative measure: [action] within [timeframe] |
| **Habit Moment** | The point where usage becomes routine — "I now use [product] for [purpose]" |
| **Habit Moment Metric** | XdY format — X uses in Y days (e.g., 4d7 = 4 uses in 7 days) |
| **Engagement Metric** | The ongoing metric tracking depth/breadth of engagement |

## Case Study: Pinterest {#pinterest}

| Field | Use Case 1: Browsing Interests | Use Case 2: Planning A Project |
|-------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Problem** | I'm bored | I have a project and need a lot of inspiration quickly |
| **Persona** | Female, Millennial, $30K+ Income | Female, Planning Life Event, Millennial+, $50K+ Income |
| **Why** | I can browse pictures around my interests | I can find things on Pinterest I can't find anywhere else |
| **Alternative** | Browsing magazines | Google Images, Asking Experts |
| **Frequency** | Weekly | Daily then disappears when project is finished |
| **Retention Metric** | WAR (Weekly Active Return) | DAR (Daily Active Return) |
| **Setup Moment** | I've found some topics that interest me | I can see a bunch of different options and then narrow down quickly |
| **Setup Metric** | Follow 5 topics first day | Use search in first day |
| **Aha Moment** | I've found something cool in an interesting feed | I've found something for my project that I like |
| **Aha Moment Metric** | First pin in first 7 days | First pin in first day |
| **Habit Moment** | "I find cool things on Pinterest every week" | "I use Pinterest as the central source of inspiration for my project" |
| **Habit Moment Metric** | 4d28r (4 days active in 28 days) | 4d7 (4 days active in 7 days) |
| **Engagement Metric** | Frequency — DAR/WAR | Intensity — Pins/DAR |

**Key insight**: Two use cases with very different frequencies (Weekly vs Daily) and different retention metrics. The "Planning a Project" use case has inherent churn risk — users leave when the project is done. This means retention strategy must focus on bridging users to new projects or converting them to the "Browsing" use case.

## Case Study: HubSpot Sales {#hubspot-sales}

| Field | Use Case 1: Sales Efficiency | Use Case 2: Sales Activity Reporting |
|-------|------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Problem** | I spend countless hours on inefficient activities like CRM data entry, manually following up, rewriting emails | I don't have granular insight into my team's activity which causes inaccurate reporting |
| **Persona** | Sales Rep in company 50–2000 employees | Sales Manager or VP Sales in company 50–2000 employees |
| **Why** | I can automate these activities which helps me close more deals | I can get deeper activity reports easily |
| **Alternative** | Picking up phone, sending reminder emails, copy & pasting | Excel or CRM wrangling |
| **Frequency** | Daily | Weekly |
| **Retention Metric** | DAU | WAU |
| **Setup Moment** | I've successfully installed and tracked an email to a real person | There are a few reps on my team that have used for a full week |
| **Setup Metric** | Sent tracked email within 2 days | 3+ Reps Installed + Tracking, 7 days |
| **Aha Moment** | Getting information on an important sales email they didn't have before | I've viewed a report that is very helpful |
| **Aha Moment Metric** | Viewed first email notification within 2 days | Viewed first full report, 7 days |
| **Habit Moment** | "I now use HubSpot Sales for almost all of my sales emails" | "I use HubSpot Sales to get my weekly reports for management meetings" |
| **Habit Moment Metric** | 4d7 (4 uses in 7 days) | 4d28 (4 uses in 28 days) |
| **Engagement Metric** | Feature — DAU Power/DAU | Frequency — DAU/WAU |

**Key insight**: The buyer (Sales Manager) and the user (Sales Rep) have completely different use cases, frequencies, and moments. The Manager's Setup Moment depends on the Rep's adoption — this creates a dependency chain. Activation strategy must start with Reps before Managers can reach their Aha Moment.

## Case Study: Airbnb {#airbnb}

| Field | Use Case 1: Guest | Use Case 2: Host |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Problem** | I need an affordable and safe place to stay when I travel | I want supplemental income |
| **Persona** | Budget traveler typically between 20 and 40 years old | Owner of 2nd home or home with extra space |
| **Why** | 30% cheaper | Meaningful additional income with low effort |
| **Alternative** | Hotels or Motels | Couchsurfing, working side jobs, other passive income |
| **Frequency** | Yearly | Weekly |
| **Retention Metric** | YAG (Yearly Active Guests) | WAH (Weekly Active Hosts) |
| **Setup Moment** | I found a cool/cheap place to stay | I have my home listed and got my booking |
| **Setup Metric** | First Night Booked, 3 Months | Accepted First Booking, 7 days |
| **Aha Moment** | Booked night turns out well | Made money while having a good experience with the guest |
| **Aha Moment Metric** | 4+ Star Review, 6 months | Gave 4+ Star Review To Guest, 1 month |
| **Habit Moment** | Booking second stay within one year | Getting second guest |
| **Habit Moment Metric** | 2b365 (2 bookings within a year) | 4b28 (4 bookings within 28 days) |
| **Engagement Metric** | Frequency — QAG/YAG (Quarterly active guests / yearly active guests) | Intensity — Bookings/WAH |

**Key insight**: Extreme frequency mismatch between two sides of the marketplace — Guests are yearly, Hosts are weekly. The Guest's Aha Moment requires an actual stay to go well, which means the time-to-value is extremely long (months). This makes pre-stay experience design critical for retention. The Host side is a cross-side network effect — more hosts = better guest experience = more guests = more bookings for hosts.

## How to Use This Template {#how-to-use}

### For Reviews (growth-review)
1. Start with the blank template
2. Fill in what you know from the user's existing data and flows
3. Mark gaps with "[UNKNOWN — need data]"
4. Compare against the case studies to identify what's missing or misaligned
5. The gaps ARE your diagnosis — each empty or weak cell points to a specific improvement area

### For Execution (growth-execution)
1. Fill in the CRC for the new feature/flow being built
2. Use case studies as calibration — are your metrics at the right level of specificity?
3. The Setup → Aha → Habit chain defines your core flow — each moment maps to a screen/step in the PRD
4. The frequency determines your engagement loop design and notification strategy

### Common Mistakes
- **Vague moments**: "User finds value" is not an Aha Moment. "User receives first email open notification within 2 days" is.
- **Wrong frequency**: If your retention metric doesn't match natural use frequency, you'll misdiagnose retention health. A yearly product (like Airbnb for guests) with a DAU retention metric looks terrible.
- **Ignoring buyer/user split**: In B2B, the buyer and user often have different CRCs (see HubSpot example). Design for both.
- **Skipping alternatives**: Understanding what users do WITHOUT your product reveals what "good enough" looks like and what you need to beat.

*All case studies are hypothetical examples created for educational purposes using publicly available information. They do not contain proprietary data.*
